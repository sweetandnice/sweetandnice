I love Earth because it is a perfect self-contained cycle and it's all we have. As William Shatner tearfully attempted to express to Jeff Bezos after his trip to space (before Bezos dismissed him to celebrate with champagne), "The jeopardy, the moment you feel that vulnerability. This air which is keeping us alive is thinner than your skin. It’s a sliver, immeasurably small when you think in terms of the universe. Mars doesn’t really have it. There is life, and then there’s death."

Our current lifestyle under globalized capitalism is fundamentally unsustainable because it depends on the constant devaluing of labor and the environment. It only survives by externalizing the costs of its model of infinite growth—geographically by relocating production to places where labor is kept precarious through political repression and foreign‑backed ‘pro‑market’ regimes, and environmentally by degrading the ecosystem services it relies on. In 1997, Robert Costanza attempted to calculate the global monetary value of all ecosystem services , which he estimated at $33 trillion per year (more than global GDP at the time).
