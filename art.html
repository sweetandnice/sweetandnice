Sketches:

In an art and ecology course I took, I was assigned the task of turning a quick intuitive gesture made with ink and a bird's feather into a multimedia piece incorporating the feather itself, which would represent both an animal and an environmental activist that speaks to us. Instead of picking a singular activist, I chose to portray collective action by interpreting the Chipko (tree-hugging) movement, an anti-logging protest which took place in rural Himalayan highlands in the 1970's where women wrapped themselves around trees, alongside a siphonophore, a deep-sea organism that is not a single creature but a community of specialized bodies (zooids) working together as one.
