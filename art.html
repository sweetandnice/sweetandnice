Sketches:

In an art and ecology course I took, I was assigned the task of turning a quick intuitive gesture made with ink and a bird's feather into a multimedia piece incorporating the feather itself, which would represent both an animal and an environmental activist that speaks to us. Instead of picking a singular activist, I chose to portray collective action by interpreting the Chipko (tree-hugging) movement, an anti-logging protest which took place in rural Himalayan highlands in the 1970's where women wrapped themselves around trees, alongside a siphonophore, a deep-sea organism that is not a single creature but a community of specialized bodies (zooids) working together as one. In my final piece, I used pens, markers, colored pencils, and acrylic paint. I put the feather hanging off the side of the canvas, blending into the black center of the tree rings. I chose to keep the text in Hindi as a stylistic and symbolic choice. However, because of my unfamiliarability with the script, I ended up having to clean it back up and re-do it. While there were elements of the original piece that I like, the acrylic paint and sloppy typography cheapened the whole thing for me.
